RK3399 DW-HDMI register spacing in this tree
========================================

The DW-HDMI controller on RK3399 is byte-addressed logically, but the MMIO
window exposed here uses 32-bit spacing between logical registers. Direct
helpers must access HDMI register N as base[N * 4], not base[N].

Why this matters:
- old direct helpers in this repo were reading and writing the wrong offsets
- that produced false all-zero snapshots and fake write failures
- PHY/VOP debugging only became consistent after fixing the stride

Rule for direct tools:
- use helper macros/functions such as HR(o)/HW(o,v) or g_hdmi[o * 4]
- do not index the DW-HDMI block as g_hdmi[o] or hdmi[o]
